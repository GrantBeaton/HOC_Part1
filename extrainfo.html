A bunch of other cool stuff

1

Jack Kirby was not the only one working on the integrated circuit, he received the Nobel Prize as a co-recipient with two men named Herbert Kroemer and Zhores Alferov.

2

He didn't only invent the integrated circuit, as he was actually awarded over 50 patents that he was awarded. This includes things like the first IC-designed computer for the U.S Air Force in 1961 and a semiconductor based thermal printer in 1965.

3

Another invention of his that is almost as important as his invention of the microchip of today was the first IC-powered calculator, the Pocketronic, which earned him the patent that supports all modern day calculators.

4

He worked lots of jobs, mainly at Texas Instruments, where he did all of his inventing, but between 1978-84, he worked as a teacher at Texas A&M University.

5

For his work, he did not just earn a Nobel Prize, but he also received the National Medal of Science, the Charles Stark Draper Medal and the National Medal of Technology.

Truly an underappreciated genius
